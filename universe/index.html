What

We are building the first human programming language: a creative tool for people, not programmers. This language is open-ended like LEGO. Building blocks, not templates. And it's designed for mobile devices: the computers we all have.
It starts with a new kind of canvas. We call them cards. Not apps or websites, but cards. And anyone can make them—no coding required. Cards run natively on your phone, but they can link to each other and be embedded anywhere.
The cards you make aren't floating in space. They exist in a network called Universe.
Universe isn't a single product or destination. It's a set of tools and a community designed to evolve what the world can do with the web.

Who

We are a group of designers & programmers, musicians & mathematicians, artists & philosophers. We come from companies like Google, Khan Academy, and Kickstarter. We have built startups before.
We're students of thinkers old and new—Alan Kay, Seymour Papert, Doug Engelbart, Ted Nelson, Bill Atkinson, Stewart Brand, Bret Victor — and their creations—Hypercard, Smalltalk, Mindstorms, the Web.
Computers at their best decentralize power — they liberate the individual and facilitate collective intelligence. The devices have become mainstream but this profound freedom has not. We want to change that.
This is a dream project for each of us: a way to apply our learnings—and dreams—towards a better future for computing.
We're 7 people based in Brooklyn, NY. We've raised capital from premier investors, so we have the resources we need to make this dream real. Now we need you.

How

This is our work-in-progress, constantly evolving, collective constitution. The principles that inform the product and how we work.


Our Beliefs

  • We believe that curiosity and creativity drive the world forward. At our loftiest, we endeavor to foster and stimulate these innate human characteristics.
  • We believe in the simple alchemy of exchanging ideas, art, information.
  • We believe in the power and primacy of the network. That the world, like the Internet, is at its best when everyone contributes to it.
  • We believe that humans are fundamentally creators. The world around us is made, and made by people.
  • We believe in the infinite potentialities of technology to positively transform life as we know it.

Our Values

  • Excellence.
    Relentlessly pursue greatness in quality, performance, and work. Don't measure performance by others' standards. March to your own beat and set the bar as high as you can see.
  • Imagination.
    Dream big to build a better future. Creativity is inherently unprecedented so don't be afraid of being radical: reject normal, break rules, be fearless.
  • Honesty.
    Speak your mind. Embrace radical transparency. In order to act as one mind and do our best work we must strive for truth. Honesty is a sign of respect.
  • Collaboration.
    The whole is greater than the some of its parts. Our team is our greatest asset; put the needs of the collective ahead of individual ones.
  • Beauty.
    Great technology is not enough. We obsess about the way our work looks and feels. Beauty delights the soul; it makes change exciting. Surround yourself with the best to refine your taste.
  • Optimism.
    We believe that tomorrow will be better than today. This isn't about avoiding negativity, it is about focusing on improvement. Look at every situation and focus on how you can make it better.
  • Evolution.
    Commit to personal growth, especially when it's uncomfortable. It's always possible to be better. With ravenous curiosity and strong determination we'll become our best selves.
  • Determination.
    To accomplish what people think is impossible, we must try harder. Find a solution when none seems to exist.
  • Focus.
    Say no to say yes. With limited resources and high standards, choosing how we spend our time becomes our most important decision.
  • Fun!
    We are human. We think of work as play. And our work is our art.

Why

Pocket-sized supercomputers have powered a paradigm shift in how we learn, connect, and communicate. But what can we actually make with phones? The answer is not much.
The tools we do have to express ourselves and explore online feel shrunken, primitive: imposing limits instead of ripping open opportunity and unleashing new forms of creativity. 140 characters, 6 seconds, 150 friends.
The digital world shapes life as we know it. But as the devices we use every day have advanced in complexity and scope, the power to shape that world is limited to a few, and the world is worse off for it.
We think that if everyone had the power to build things with technology—even if they don’t program—the world would be an infinitely better, richer, and more interesting place.

“We shape our tools and then our tools shape us.”
